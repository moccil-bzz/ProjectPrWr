The introduction of the reforms

At the beginning of 1979, many changes were made in the economy. Farmers could now sell a portion of their harvest on the open market. Special zones were established on the coast for the purpose of foreign trade, leading to an increase in exports and imports.

Economic control was also transferred to local and provincial governments in many sectors, which then operated under free-market principles rather than a state-planned economy. People were also encouraged to start their own businesses. State-set prices were largely eliminated as well.

This liberalization of the economy was the key to China's massive growth. The gradual introduction of reforms allowed them to determine what worked and what did not, enabling the preferred policies to be implemented throughout the rest of the country.
