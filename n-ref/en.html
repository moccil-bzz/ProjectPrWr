Development post introduction

After the reforms were introduced, economic growth increased significantly, and larger economic disruptions were avoided, except for the Tiananmen Square massacre in June 1989, where their economy was heavily impacted, especially due to foreign sanctions. However, these sanctions were lifted in 1991, and their growth rebounded.

Afterward, their real GDP (Gross Domestic Product adjusted for inflation - the value of all goods or services produced by a country within a year) had an average annual increase of 9.5% between 1979 and 2018, meaning they doubled the size of their economy approximately every eight years on average.

The 2008 financial crisis also had negative consequences for China. Twenty million workers who were employed abroad returned after losing their jobs. To counter the crisis, they invested 586 billion dollars, focusing on infrastructure and easing monetary policies to increase borrowing from banks.
